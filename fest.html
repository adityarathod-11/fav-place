Yavatmal is known for its vibrant and enthusiastic Navratri celebrations, particularly its Durga Puja. The city is famous for its large number of Durga idols and pandals, with over 500 registered pandals in a town of 10 lakh people. The Main Line area of Yavatmal is particularly known for its colorful and lively Navratri celebrations.

Gudi Padwa, the Marathi New Year, is celebrated on the first day of the Chaitra month, which falls in March or April. In Yavatmal, like other parts of Maharashtra, the festival is marked by the raising of the Gudi (a decorated flag) and various traditional customs.

Pola, also known as Bail Pola, is a vibrant agricultural festival celebrated by farmers in Maharashtra, primarily in the month of Shravana, to honor the bullocks and oxen that play a crucial role in their farming activities. The festival involves decorating the animals with colorful clothes, garlands, and ornaments, and offering them special food. It's a day of rest and gratitude for the animals, with farmers also taking the opportunity to worship them.

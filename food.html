A festive Maharashtrian classic, Puran Poli is a golden, ghee-roasted flatbread stuffed with a sweet mixture of jaggery and chana dal. Soft, rich, and full of warmth, it melts in your mouth — often enjoyed during Holi, Gudi Padwa, and Diwali.

A Vidarbha gem, Shev Bhaji is a spicy, soulful curry made with crisp sev soaked in a rich red gravy. It’s fiery, flavorful, and best paired with pav or chapati — a staple in local homes and messes.

Crispy outside and soft inside, Anarsa is a delicate sweet made from fermented rice flour and jaggery, coated with poppy seeds and deep-fried to golden perfection. It’s a traditional treat that brings festive joy with every bite.

Straight from the heart of Vidarbha, Varhadi Chicken is a fiery, rustic curry made with earthy spices and black pepper. Bursting with bold flavors, it’s the perfect match for bhakri or rice — a true reflection of the region’s intense food culture.

Simple yet satisfying, Anda Rice is spicy fried rice tossed with eggs, onions, chilies, and local masalas. It’s a go-to comfort food in Yavatmal, often enjoyed at roadside stalls and college canteens.

A beloved street breakfast in Yavatmal, Tarri Poha is soft, flattened rice layered with a bold, spicy “tarri” (red chili gravy). Garnished with sev, onion, and lemon, it’s a fiery yet comforting dish that wakes up your taste buds with every bite.
